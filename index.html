Elliptic Curve Lab

Exploring the mathematical foundations of ECDLP — not to break cryptography, but to understand why it works.

Welcome to Elliptic Curve Lab — a long-term research project dedicated to understanding the Elliptic Curve Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP). We explore the mathematical structures, algorithms, and theoretical limits that make elliptic curve cryptography secure.

Our goal is not to find practical attacks on Bitcoin or other systems. Instead, we seek to map the "space of all possible speedups" and understand the fundamental barrier that prevents classical algorithms from solving ECDLP faster than $O(\sqrt{n})$.

Research Areas

Curve Models

Weierstrass, Montgomery, Edwards — how different representations affect arithmetic complexity and security.

Isogenies & Endomorphisms

Mappings between curves, GLV endomorphisms, complex multiplication, and their implications for ECDLP.

Attack Algorithms

Pollard's rho, Pohlig-Hellman, index calculus variants, MOV attack, and summation polynomials.

Theoretical Limits

Why is sub-$\sqrt{n}$ so hard? Exploring no-go theorems and the geometry of the problem.

Research Values

Formal Proofs

Every claim is supported by rigorous mathematical argumentation — no speculation.

Reproducible Experiments

All code is open-source. Results can be verified and built upon by the community.

Negative Results Matter

Dead ends are documented thoroughly — knowing what doesn't work is valuable knowledge.

Open Science

Research reports, blog posts, and code are freely available for everyone.
