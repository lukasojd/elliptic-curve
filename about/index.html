About

Elliptic Curve Lab is a personal research blog and knowledge archive focused on elliptic curve mathematics and cryptography. The primary purpose is to document research progress, preserve insights, and share knowledge with the community.

Purpose

This site serves as:

  • A research notebook for ongoing elliptic curve studies
  • A knowledge base preserving mathematical insights and discoveries
  • A collection of implementation notes and optimization techniques
  • A reference for future work and collaboration

Topics

Research areas covered include:

  • Elliptic curve group structure and arithmetic
  • Coordinate systems (affine, projective, Jacobian, Montgomery)
  • Scalar multiplication algorithms
  • Endomorphisms and isogenies
  • The discrete logarithm problem and related algorithms
  • Cryptographic protocols (ECDSA, ECDH, etc.)
  • Efficient implementation techniques

Mathematical Background

An elliptic curve over a field $K$ is defined by the Weierstrass equation:

$$y^2 = x^3 + ax + b$$

where $4a^3 + 27b^2 \neq 0$ (non-singular condition). The set of points forms an abelian group with the point at infinity as identity.
