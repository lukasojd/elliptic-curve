About This Project

What We Do

Elliptic Curve Lab is a long-term research program focused on understanding the Elliptic Curve Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP). We study the mathematical structures underlying elliptic curve cryptography — with special attention to the secp256k1 curve used in Bitcoin.

Our goal is not to break real-world cryptography. Instead, we seek deep mathematical understanding: Why is ECDLP so hard? What makes $O(\sqrt{n})$ the barrier? Are there hidden structures that could — in theory — be exploited?

The Fundamental Question

After decades of research, no known classical algorithm can solve ECDLP on a general curve faster than $O(\sqrt{n})$, where $n$ is the group order. For a 256-bit curve, this means roughly $2^{128} \approx 3.4 \times 10^{38}$ operations — completely infeasible.

We ask: Why? Is there a deeper geometric or algebraic reason? Can we formulate no-go theorems that explain this barrier? Or is there some overlooked structure that could provide a shortcut?

What We Study

  • Curve models: Weierstrass, Montgomery, Edwards, and their computational trade-offs
  • Isogenies: Mappings between curves and their security implications
  • Endomorphisms: GLV optimization, complex multiplication, automorphism groups
  • Attack algorithms: Pollard's rho, Pohlig-Hellman, index calculus, MOV attack
  • Special structures: Curves with $j$-invariant 0, CM curves, embedding degree
  • Theoretical limits: Lower bounds, generic group model, no-go arguments

Research Values

Formal Correctness

Every claim is backed by rigorous mathematical proof or clear justification. We don't speculate — we prove, or we say we don't know.

Reproducible Experiments

We develop algorithm prototypes, measure their performance, and publish all code. Anyone can verify our results and build upon them.

Negative Results Are Valuable

When an approach doesn't work, we document exactly why it failed. Systematic refutation of dead ends is part of our mission — it saves others from repeating the same mistakes.

Open Science

All outputs — research reports, blog articles, source code — are freely available. This supports credibility and enables collaboration.

Long-Term Vision

This is not a single paper or a quick project. We aim to systematically map the entire "space of possible speedups" for ECDLP — every known technique, every theoretical limit, every dead end. The goal is to understand, once and for all, what makes this problem hard.

If we find that no sub-$\sqrt{n}$ breakthrough is possible, we want to explain why convincingly. If we discover something new — even impractical — it advances our understanding of one of cryptography's most important problems.
